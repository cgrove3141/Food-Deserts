About Food Deserts

A food desert is an urban or rural area where the availability of affordable, fresh, healthy food is limited. While food deserts can be defined in several ways, generally, a food desert is defined by some measure of distance (e.g. 1 mile) to access from a supermarket. The issue of food deserts is an ethical one, as residents of these areas tend to be more likely to have diet-related health issues such as obesity.

Our team has taken a look at a dataset released by the USDA on food deserts and developed visualizations related to these deserts. We have applied a Machine Learning model to the data to be able to predict where food deserts may exist when the 2020 Census data is released. We have also applied this tool backwards to see how food deserts may have evolved over time.
